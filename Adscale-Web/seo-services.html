Common SEO Problems

Is Your Website
Struggling With SEO?

These are the most common SEO challenges businesses face. If any of these sound familiar, a structured SEO approach can help.

🚫
Not on Google
Your website exists but does not appear in search results. Pages may not be indexed or discoverable by search engines.
📉
Low Organic Traffic
Your website receives minimal visitors from search engines despite having content and products to offer.
🎯
Wrong Keywords
You are targeting search terms that your ideal customers are not actually using to find your services.
🏁
Competitors Rank Higher
Your competitors consistently appear above you, capturing the traffic and leads your business should be getting.
🔧
Technical Errors
Broken links, crawl errors, server issues, and poor site structure prevent search engines from properly indexing your website.
🐢
Slow Website
Slow loading speeds hurt your rankings and drive visitors away. Google prioritizes fast-loading websites in search results.
📱
Poor Mobile Experience
Your site does not work well on mobile devices, which affects rankings since Google uses mobile-first indexing.
📄
Pages Not Indexed
Important pages on your website are not being added to Google's index, making them invisible in search results.
✏️
Weak Content
Your content does not address what users are searching for, lacks depth, or is duplicated across multiple pages.
📍
Inconsistent Listings
Your business name, address, and phone number vary across directories, confusing search engines and reducing local visibility.
🔗
Low-Quality Backlinks
Your website has few or poor-quality backlinks, limiting its authority and ability to rank competitively.
📋
No Clear Reporting
You have no visibility into what is happening with your SEO — no data, no insights, no direction for improvement.
📭
Traffic Without Leads
You get visitors but they do not convert into enquiries, calls, or sales. Traffic alone does not grow a business.
📊
Sudden Ranking Drops
Your rankings dropped unexpectedly. Google algorithm updates, technical issues, or competitor changes could be the cause.
🧭
No Long-Term Strategy
SEO efforts are inconsistent or reactive. Without a structured plan, sustainable results remain out of reach.

FAQs

Common Questions
About Our SEO Services.

Honest answers to the questions we hear most often from business owners exploring SEO for the first time.

What is SEO?
SEO stands for Search Engine Optimization. It is the practice of improving your website to increase its visibility when people search for products or services related to your business on Google and other search engines. The goal is to attract relevant visitors who are actively looking for what you offer.
How long does SEO take to show results?
SEO is a medium-to-long-term strategy. Most businesses start seeing meaningful improvements in 3 to 6 months. Technical fixes may show impact sooner, while content and authority building takes longer. Results depend on your website's current state, competition, and the scope of work required.
Can you guarantee first-page rankings?
No ethical SEO agency can guarantee specific rankings. Google's algorithm considers hundreds of factors, many of which are outside our control. We focus on improving your website's quality, relevance, and authority — which gives you the best possible chance of ranking well over time.
What is included in an SEO audit?
An SEO audit reviews your website's technical health, indexing status, crawlability, page speed, mobile usability, metadata, heading structure, content quality, keyword targeting, backlink profile, local SEO signals, and competitor positioning. It identifies what is working and what needs improvement.
How do you select keywords?
We select keywords based on relevance to your business, search intent (what the user wants), competition level, and potential to drive qualified traffic. We prioritize terms that align with your services and that real customers are using to find businesses like yours.
What is technical SEO?
Technical SEO refers to optimizing your website's infrastructure so search engines can crawl, index, and understand your content effectively. It includes site speed, mobile responsiveness, XML sitemaps, robots.txt, canonical tags, structured data, redirects, and HTTPS security.
What is on-page SEO?
On-page SEO involves optimizing individual web pages to rank higher and earn more relevant traffic. This includes SEO titles, meta descriptions, heading structure, keyword placement, URL optimization, internal linking, image alt text, content formatting, and schema markup.
What is off-page SEO?
Off-page SEO refers to activities outside your website that influence your search rankings. This primarily includes building high-quality backlinks from reputable websites, earning brand mentions, local citations, and digital PR. We focus on quality and relevance, not volume.
Do you provide local SEO?
Yes. Local SEO is a core part of our services. We optimize Google Business Profiles, ensure NAP consistency across directories, target local keywords, build local citations, and improve Google Maps visibility for businesses that serve specific geographic areas.
Can you optimize my Google Business Profile?
Yes. We optimize your Google Business Profile with accurate business information, appropriate categories, relevant keywords, service descriptions, photos, and regular updates. We also provide guidance on managing reviews and engaging with customer questions.
Do you create SEO content?
Yes. Content is an essential part of SEO. We develop content strategies based on keyword research, create content briefs, and can write or guide the creation of SEO-optimized pages, blog posts, FAQs, and service descriptions that address what your customers are searching for.
Do you build backlinks?
We build backlinks through ethical, quality-focused methods — including content-driven outreach, digital PR, broken link building, and relevant business citations. We never buy links, use private blog networks, or engage in spammy link-building practices.
How do you ensure backlink quality?
We evaluate every potential backlink source for domain authority, relevance to your industry, traffic quality, and editorial standards. We avoid links from directories, link farms, and unrelated sites. Quality and relevance always matter more than the number of backlinks.
Will you need access to my website?
Certain technical and on-page SEO tasks require website access. We request limited, role-based access only for the specific platforms we need to work on. We follow strict security protocols and never make changes without your approval.
Do you use Google Analytics and Search Console?
Yes. Google Analytics and Google Search Console are essential tools we use to track performance, identify issues, and measure progress. We set them up properly, ensure accurate tracking, and use the data to guide our SEO decisions and monthly reporting.
What will I receive in monthly reports?
Monthly reports include keyword position changes, organic traffic trends, search impressions and clicks, landing page performance, technical issues resolved, content completed, backlink updates, local SEO progress, and a clear action plan for the next month — all explained in simple business language.
Can you optimize a newly launched website?
Yes. New websites benefit greatly from SEO from day one. We can ensure proper technical setup, optimize pages for target keywords, set up analytics and Search Console, create a content plan, and build a foundation for long-term organic growth.
Can you fix SEO issues on an existing website?
Yes. We regularly work with existing websites to identify and resolve SEO issues — including technical problems, content gaps, indexing errors, duplicate content, slow pages, broken links, and declining rankings. We start with a thorough audit to understand what needs to be fixed.
Is SEO better than paid advertising?
SEO and paid advertising serve different purposes. SEO builds long-term, sustainable visibility without ongoing ad spend. Paid ads deliver immediate traffic but stop when you stop paying. Most successful businesses use both — SEO for long-term growth and paid ads for short-term campaigns.
Can SEO and Google Ads work together?
Yes. SEO and Google Ads complement each other effectively. SEO builds organic visibility for long-term traffic, while Google Ads can target specific keywords with immediate visibility. Running both allows you to dominate search results and capture traffic at every stage of the customer journey.
